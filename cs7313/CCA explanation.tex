Let $W_{DS} \mem \br^{|V_{DS}| \times d_1}$ and $W_G \mem \br^{|V_G| \times d_2}$ be the domain specific and generic word embedding matrices.

Let $w_{i, DS}$ and $w_{i, G}$ be the embedding of the word i \mem V$_\inn$ \eql V$_{DS}$ \inn V$_G$

Let $\phi_{DS}$ and $\phi_G$ be the projection directions of $w_{i, DS}$ and $w_{i, G}$, respectively.

\

For each embedding of each word, we will project it like so (the $\phi$'s are like the a and b in the CCA tutorial):

\begin{equation*}
	\overline w_{i, DS} = w_{i, DS} \; \phi_{DS} \; \mathrm{ and } \; \overline w_{i, G} = w_{i, G} \; \phi_{G}
\end{equation*}
and we'll maximize the correlation of this projection like so:
\begin{equation*}
	\rho(\phi_{DS}, \phi_G) \eql \max \frac{\mathbb{E}[<\overline w_{i, DS}, \overline w_{i, G}>]}{\sqrt{\mathbb{E}[\overline w^2_{i, DS}]\;\mathbb{E}[\overline w^2_{i, G}]}}
\end{equation*}

We can repeat this process up to d \eql min(d$_1$, d$_2$) times, such that each canonical variable is uncorrelated with the previous ones.

\

Once we do that, we have two matrices of projection vectors: $\boldsymbol\phi_{DS}$ \mem $\br^{d_1 \times d}$ and $\boldsymbol\phi_{G}$ \mem $\br^{d_2 \times d}$

\

Finally, for a particular word i, we achieve its domain-adapted word embedding like so:
\begin{equation*}
	w_{i, DA} = \frac{1}{2} w_{i, DS} \; \boldsymbol\phi_{DS} + \frac{1}{2} w_{i, G}\; \boldsymbol\phi_{G} 
\end{equation*}
