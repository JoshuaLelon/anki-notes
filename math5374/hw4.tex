6.1

\

If P is an orthogonal projector, then I \ms 2P is unitary. Prove this algebraically, and give a geometric interpretation.

\

In other words, we want to show:

\begin{displaymath}
  (I - 2P)(I - 2P)^* = I
\end{displaymath}

Recall that if P is an orthogonal projector, then P \eql \uf{P}{*}.

\begin{displaymath}
\splt{
(I - 2P)(I - 2P)^* & \\
& = (I - 2P)(I - 2P^*) \\ 
& = I^2 - 2P - 2P^* + 4PP^* \\
& = I^2 - 4P + 4PP \\
& = I^2 - 4P + 4P \\
& = I^2 \\
& = I
}
\end{displaymath}

Hence, I \ms 2P is unitary.

\

The geometric interpretation is this:

I \ms P is a comp projector to P. Since they project onto opposite spaces and I \ms P is already unitary, scaling the vectors subtracted from I doesn't change the direction, which still makes it orthogonal.

6.3

\

Give A \mem \uf{\bc}{m \times n} with m \gre n, show that \uf{A}{*}A is nonsingular if and only if A has full rank.

\

\rar (beginning with \uf{A}{*}A is non-singular)

\uf{A}{*}A is non-singular implies that

\begin{displaymath}
  \sigma_n \gre \sigma_{n - 1} \gre ... \gre \sigma_1 \gr 0
\end{displaymath}

By Theorem 5.4, $\sigma$'s of A are the square roots of \uf{A}{*}A.

Thus, 

\begin{displaymath}
  \sigma_i = \sqrt{\lambda_i}
\end{displaymath}

and

\begin{displaymath}
  \sigma_i^2 = \lambda_i > 0
\end{displaymath}

for i := 1 ... n

Since all n eigenvalues are greater than 0 and m \gre n, A has full rank.

\

\lar (beginning with A has full rank)

\

A has full rank implies that

\begin{displaymath}
  \lambda_n \gre \lambda_{n - 1} \gre ... \gre \lambda_1 \gr 0
\end{displaymath}

Notice:

\begin{displaymath}
\splt{
  A^* A & = (XVX^{-1})^* XVX^{-1} \\
  & (X^{-1})^* V X^* X V X^{-1}
}
\end{displaymath}
